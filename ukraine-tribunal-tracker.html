About This Tracker

Special Tribunal for the Crime of Aggression (ST-CA)

The Special Tribunal for the Crime of Aggression against Ukraine is an internationally agreed judicial body being established to prosecute individuals most responsible for the crime of aggression against Ukraine following Russia's full-scale invasion on 24 February 2022. The tribunal has jurisdiction over acts constituting the crime of aggression as defined under international law. It was developed through a core group process facilitated by the Council of Europe and supported by the EU and partner nations. The Agreement was opened for signature at a ceremony in Strasbourg and requires a minimum number of ratifications to enter into force.

International Claims Commission / Register of Damage (ICCR / RD4U)

The Register of Damage Caused by the Aggression of the Russian Federation against Ukraine (RD4U) was established by the Council of Europe's Committee of Ministers and inaugurated at the Reykjavik Summit in May 2023. It records claims and evidence of damage caused by Russia's aggression, forming the evidential foundation for the International Claims Commission for Ukraine (ICCR) — the full reparations mechanism intended to assess and award compensation to victims. The governing agreement (CETS No. 248) was opened for signature in 2023. The ICCR, once operational with sufficient ratifications, will process registered claims.

Status Definitions

  • Ratified — The state has completed all domestic legal processes and deposited its instrument of ratification with the depositary (Council of Europe Secretary General).
  • Signed — A minister or ambassador has signed the agreement, expressing intent to ratify. Domestic parliamentary processes may be ongoing.
  • Supporting — The state has publicly committed political support but has not yet formally signed the agreement.
  • No formal action — No public commitment or signature recorded at the time of this update.

Alert Logic

A country is flagged (⚠️ Not joined) when it is a member of the European Union or the Council of Europe and has not yet deposited a ratification instrument or signed the respective agreement. These states are expected to participate given their existing commitments to the rule of law and the European legal order.
